Panama's flavor

One week in Panama was not enough to enjoy all the wonderful landscapes this country has to offer. We felt like little kids in an ice-cream factory!

Our trip to Panama was quite spontaneous, like almost all nice trips we've done!

Our plan was flying from Germany to Colombia as cheap as possible. We figured out that if we did a short stop in Panama, the cost would decrease reasonably. Many people book direct flights to save time, but we wanted to save money and why not travelling more.

We checked out the options and found out that the price was the same for a 12 hours stop as it was for one week stop, so we booked our adventure including one week in Panama for the same price!

Despite Panama City has many things to offer to nature lovers like us, we didn't spend much time there. Next time, for sure!

I had seen beautiful pics from Bocas del Toro so we planned most of our stay there.

We flew from Germany to Panama City, then from Panama City to David. There, we took a small regional bus from David to Almirante

The way from David to Almirante was really nice. The view was spectacular! I am from a Caribbean country but still it was fascinating to me, see how the jungle and the sea live in an incredible harmony there.

Our expectations were quite high for the short time but Panama didn't disappoint us.

The bus drops you off in Almirante. There you can take a ride to the place where the boots are located. If you are local you get a discount for the boot trip.

We stayed three days in Isla Solarte and three days in Bocas.

For the short stay, these islands offered us a tasteful tropical experience!
